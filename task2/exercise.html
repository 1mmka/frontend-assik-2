Yuri Gagarin: First Human in Space

1934 - 1968

About Yuri Gagarin

Yuri Gagarin was a Soviet pilot and cosmonaut who became the first human to journey into outer space in 1961. Born in 1934 in the village of Klushino, Russia, he quickly rose through the ranks of the Soviet Air Force. His historic flight aboard Vostok 1 made him an international hero and a symbol of Soviet space achievement. Gagarin's legacy continues to inspire generations of explorers and scientists. He tragically died in a jet crash in 1968 at the age of 34.

Major Life Events

  1. 1934: Born in Klushino, Russia
  2. 1955: Graduated from technical school as a foundryman
  3. 1957: Became a Soviet Air Force pilot
  4. 1960: Selected for the Soviet space program
  5. 1961: First human in space aboard Vostok 1
  6. 1968: Died in a jet crash

Key Contributions

  • First human to orbit the Earth
  • Symbol of international space exploration
  • Promoted science and technology education
  • Inspired future generations of astronauts
